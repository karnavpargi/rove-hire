1. Definition of Confidential Information

“Confidential Information” refers to all non-public information disclosed by the Company to the Recipient, whether orally, in writing, or by any other means, including but not limited to:

  1. Trade secrets, proprietary data, business strategies, and operational processes;
  2. Software code, algorithms, technical designs, system architectures, and documentation;
  3. Financial information, pricing models, client lists, and partnership agreements;
  4. Employee data, organizational structures, and internal communications;
  5. Any information designated as “confidential” or that reasonably should be understood as confidential given its nature and circumstances of disclosure.
2. Obligations of Receiving Party

The Recipient agrees to:

  1. Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent from the Company;
  2. Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating or performing services related to potential or actual employment with the Company;
  3. Take all reasonable precautions to prevent unauthorized disclosure, including applying at minimum the same degree of care used to protect their own confidential information;
  4. Immediately notify the Company of any unauthorized use or disclosure of Confidential Information;
  5. Return or destroy all Confidential Information, including copies, upon termination of discussions or employment, or upon written request by the Company.
3. Exclusions

This Agreement does not apply to information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Recipient;
  2. Was already known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, as evidenced by written records;
  3. Is independently developed by the Recipient without use of the Confidential Information;
  4. Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order, provided that the Recipient gives the Company reasonable advance notice to seek protective measures.
4. Duration

The obligations of confidentiality shall remain in effect for a period of two (2) years from the date of disclosure of the Confidential Information, or until the information ceases to qualify as confidential under the exclusions above, whichever occurs first.

5. Remedies

The Recipient acknowledges that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm to the Company, for which monetary damages alone may be insufficient. The Company shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief and any other remedies available at law or in equity without the requirement of posting a bond.

6. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with applicable laws. Any disputes arising from or related to this Agreement shall be resolved through binding arbitration in the jurisdiction where the Company is headquartered.
